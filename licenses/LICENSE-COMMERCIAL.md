# Commercial Licensing Inquiry

*Version 1.0 — April 2026*

## Overview

Vigil is currently licensed exclusively under the [GNU General Public License v3.0 only](../LICENSE) (GPL-3.0-only). **There is no commercial license available at this time.**

This document serves as the inquiry pathway for organizations or individuals whose use cases may be incompatible with the GPL. If your situation requires alternative licensing terms, the maintainer is open to discussing options.

## When You Might Need Alternative Licensing

You may need to explore alternative licensing if you intend to:

- **Embed in proprietary software** — Incorporate Vigil (or substantial portions of it) into closed-source products or services without disclosing your source code as required by the GPL.
- **Redistribute without GPL obligations** — Distribute modified versions of Vigil to customers or end users without extending GPL rights or providing source code.
- **Comply with organizational policy** — Your organization requires non-copyleft licensing for all software dependencies.
- **Offer Vigil as a service** — Build a hosted service or managed offering around Vigil without GPL source-disclosure obligations.

## When You Do NOT Need Alternative Licensing

- **Using Vigil as an end user** — Running Vigil to monitor your own file integrity is fully covered by the GPL. No alternative license is needed.
- **Internal use with source availability** — Using Vigil within your organization, even in production, is covered by the GPL as long as you comply with its terms when distributing.
- **Contributing to Vigil** — Contributing to the project is governed by the [Contributor License Agreement](CONTRIBUTOR-LICENSE.md), not commercial licensing.

## Current Status

As of April 2026, Vigil is a single-licensed open-source project. No commercial license terms have been established. The project's licensing framework is structured to support potential future dual-licensing without requiring changes to the contributor agreement or legal infrastructure.

If commercial licensing is introduced in the future, this document will be updated with full terms, replacing this inquiry pathway with a formal license agreement.

## What Commercial Terms Could Look Like

While no commercial license currently exists, this section outlines the general framework that would be used if one were introduced. This is **informational only** and does not constitute an offer or agreement.

### Typical Grant Structure

A commercial license would grant:

- **Non-exclusive** rights — The licensor retains the right to license the software to others.
- **Worldwide** validity — In all jurisdictions.
- **Perpetual** duration (unless terminated) — For the version(s) licensed.
- **Non-transferable** rights (unless agreed otherwise) — Granted to the named licensee.

### Typical Restrictions

A commercial license would typically **not** grant the right to:

**(a)** **Redistribute Vigil as a standalone product** — It must be incorporated into a larger product or service.

**(b)** **Use the Vigil or lousclues trademarks** — Trademark rights are separate. See [TRADEMARKS.md](../TRADEMARKS.md) for trademark usage. A separate trademark license may be negotiated if needed.

**(c)** **Claim authorship** — You may not represent yourself as the author or copyright holder of the Vigil software.

**(d)** **Remove copyright notices** — All copyright notices, SPDX identifiers, and attribution must be preserved in source code and documentation, even in modified versions.
