## Succession Plan

### Why This Exists

Single-maintainer projects need continuity rules in writing.
This section defines what happens during absence or transfer.

### Scenario 1: Temporary Absence

If maintainer is unavailable for a period but expected to return:
- repository remains available under GPL-3.0-only
- community forks may continue independently
- pending PRs/issues may wait until return

### Scenario 2: Permanent Unavailability

If maintainer becomes permanently unavailable:

**Open source continuity**
- GPL-3.0-only grants are irrevocable for published code
- anyone may fork and continue development under GPL terms
- continuation may require different branding/name

**Project control continuity**
- no new centralized maintainer authority exists unless designated publicly
- community continuation may proceed via independent governance in forks

### Scenario 3: Voluntary Transfer

Maintainer can voluntarily transfer stewardship by publishing a governance update that names:
- successor maintainer or group
- scope of authority transfer
- effective date
- any transitional constraints

Any formal transfer must be documented in this file and reflected in repository permissions.
